Why Dryer Vent Cleaning Is Critical for Your Safety

According to the U.S. Fire Administration, there are approximately 2,900 home dryer fires reported each year, causing an estimated 5 deaths, 100 injuries, and $35 million in property loss. The leading cause? Failure to clean the dryer vent.

Lint and debris accumulate in your dryer vent over time, restricting airflow and creating a serious fire hazard. A clogged dryer vent also forces your dryer to work harder, increasing energy consumption and wear on the appliance.

Our Dryer Vent Cleaning Process

  • Vent Inspection - We inspect the entire dryer vent run from the dryer connection to the exterior exhaust, checking for blockages, damage, and proper installation.
  • Professional Cleaning - Using specialized rotary brush systems and high-powered vacuum equipment, we remove all lint, debris, and blockages from the entire vent line.
  • Airflow Verification - We verify proper airflow after cleaning and confirm the exterior vent flap is operating correctly.
  • Safety Report - We provide recommendations for any issues found, such as crushed vent lines, improper materials, or excessive vent length.

Warning Signs You Need Dryer Vent Cleaning

  • Clothes take longer than one cycle to dry
  • The dryer or laundry room feels excessively hot
  • A burning smell when the dryer runs
  • Lint accumulating around the dryer or vent connection
  • The exterior vent flap does not open when the dryer is running
  • It has been more than one year since your last cleaning

Benefits of Regular Dryer Vent Cleaning

  • Fire Prevention - Remove the #1 cause of dryer fires
  • Faster Drying - Restore proper airflow so clothes dry in one cycle
  • Energy Savings - A clean vent can reduce energy usage by up to 30%
  • Appliance Longevity - Reduce strain on your dryer and extend its lifespan
  • Carbon Monoxide Safety - For gas dryers, a blocked vent can cause CO buildup
